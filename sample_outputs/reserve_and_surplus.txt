Reserve and SurplusReserves and Surplus are required to be classified as:
i) Capital Reserve
ii) Capital Redemption Reserve
iii)Securities Premium Reserve
iv) Debenture Redemption Reserve
v) Revaluation Reserve
vi) Share Options Outstanding Account
vii) Other Reserves (Specifying nature and purpose)
viii) Surplus: Balance in statement of profit and loss; disclosing allocations
and Ap

v) Revaluation Reserve
vi) Share Options Outstanding Account
vii) Other Reserves (Specifying nature and purpose)
viii) Surplus: Balance in statement of profit and loss; disclosing allocations
and Appropriation such as dividend, bonus shares, transfer to/from
reserve, etc.
Significant additions/modifications regarding disclosure of reserve and surplusare as follows:
a) A reserve specifically represented by earmarked investments shall be
termed as “Fund”.
b) ‘Debit’ balance of statement of profit and loss shall be shown as a negative
figure under ‘Surplus’ head.
c) The balance of “Reserve and Surplus” after adjusting negative balance of
Surplus, if any, shall be shown under “Reserve and Surplus” read evenif the resulting figure is ‘negative’.
d) Share options outstanding account has been recognised as a separate
item under ‘Reserve and Surplus’. ICAI’s Guidance Note on Accounting
for Employee share based payments requires a credit balance in the ‘Stock

  [EASY] [conceptual] Q1:
  What is the primary purpose of classifying Reserves and Surplus in a company's balance sheet?

    A) To hide profits from shareholders
    B) To provide a clear picture of the company's financial health
    C) To reduce tax liabilities
    D) To increase the company's debt
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: B) To provide a clear picture of the company's financial health
  Why: Classifying Reserves and Surplus helps to provide a clear and transparent picture of the company's financial health.

  [MEDIUM] [scenario-based] Q2:
  A company has a debit balance of Rs. 3,00,000 in its Statement of Profit and Loss. How will this be shown in the balance sheet?

    A) As a negative figure under the Surplus head
    B) As a positive figure under the Reserve head
    C) As a negative figure under the Reserve head
    D) As a positive figure under the Surplus head
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: A) As a negative figure under the Surplus head
  Why: A debit balance in the Statement of Profit and Loss is shown as a negative figure under the Surplus head in the balance sheet.

  [MEDIUM] [definition] Q3:
  What is the meaning of the term 'Fund' in the context of Reserves and Surplus?

    A) A type of reserve that is not specifically represented by earmarked investments
    B) A type of reserve that is specifically represented by earmarked investments
    C) A type of surplus that is not available for distribution
    D) A type of surplus that is available for distribution
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: B) A type of reserve that is specifically represented by earmarked investments
  Why: A Fund refers to a reserve that is specifically represented by earmarked investments.

  [HARD] [compare/contrast] Q4:
  How do Securities Premium Reserve and Debenture Redemption Reserve differ in terms of their purpose and treatment in the balance sheet?

    A) Securities Premium Reserve is used to redeem debentures, while Debenture Redemption Reserve is used to issue bonus shares
    B) Securities Premium Reserve is used to issue bonus shares, while Debenture Redemption Reserve is used to redeem debentures
    C) Securities Premium Reserve is used to redeem preference shares, while Debenture Redemption Reserve is used to redeem debentures
    D) Securities Premium Reserve is used to issue bonus shares, while Debenture Redemption Reserve is used to redeem preference shares
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: B) Securities Premium Reserve is used to issue bonus shares, while Debenture Redemption Reserve is used to redeem debentures
  Why: Securities Premium Reserve is used to issue bonus shares, while Debenture Redemption Reserve is used to redeem debentures.

  [HARD] [true-or-false-as-MCQ] Q5:
  Is it true that a company can show a negative balance in the Reserve and Surplus section of the balance sheet?

    A) Yes, if the negative balance is due to a debit balance in the Statement of Profit and Loss
    B) No, a company can never show a negative balance in the Reserve and Surplus section
    C) Yes, if the negative balance is due to a credit balance in the Statement of Profit and Loss
    D) No, a company can only show a positive balance in the Reserve and Surplus section
