  [HARD] [conceptual] Q1:
  Which of the following is NOT required to be disclosed for each class of shares in the notes to accounts under the new disclosure requirements?

    A) The number of shares outstanding at the beginning and at the end of the reporting period.
    B) The rights, preferences and restrictions attached to the class, including restrictions on dividend distribution and repayment of capital.
    C) The aggregate number of shares held by the company's ultimate holding company and its subsidiaries.
    D) The number of shares issued, subscribed, fully paid and subscribed but not fully paid.
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: C) The aggregate number of shares held by the company's ultimate holding company and its subsidiaries.
  Why: The aggregate holdings of the ultimate holding company are disclosed separately, not per class of shares.

  [HARD] [scenario-based] Q2:
  Based on the Dinkar Ltd. scenario, what should be the amount of share capital shown in the equity section of the balance sheet?

    A) 135,90,000
    B) 136,00,000
    C) 135,70,000
    D) 135,50,000
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: A) 135,90,000
  Why: After accounting for calls received, forfeited shares, and unpaid calls, the share capital totals 135,90,000.

  [MEDIUM] [definition] Q3:
  Subscribing but not fully paid-up capital refers to:

    A) Shares that have been fully paid by the shareholders.
    B) Shares that have been subscribed but the final call has not been fully received.
    C) Shares that have been forfeited due to non-payment.
    D) Shares that have been issued but not yet subscribed.
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: B) Shares that have been subscribed but the final call has not been fully received.
  Why: It denotes shares that have been subscribed but the final payment has not been received.

  [HARD] [compare/contrast] Q4:
  Which statement best contrasts the presentation of 'Share capital' and 'Reserves and Surplus' in the balance sheet?

    A) Share capital is shown under liabilities, reserves and surplus under assets.
    B) Share capital is a component of shareholders' funds, reserves and surplus is a separate component of shareholders' funds.
    C) Share capital can be increased by issuing shares, reserves cannot.
    D) Reserves and surplus are shown in equity, share capital is shown in liabilities.
  ✗ Incorrect.
  Correct answer: B) Share capital is a component of shareholders' funds, reserves and surplus is a separate component of shareholders' funds.
  Why: Both are components of shareholders' funds, but reserves and surplus form a distinct subsection.

  [HARD] [true-or-false-as-MCQ] Q5:
